“Tatu/batok”

Tattoos have been a form of self-expression, cultural identity, and status for centuries. In the Philippines, indigenous groups used tattoos as symbols of bravery and honor, earning them the name Los Pintados ("The Painted Ones") from the Spaniards. Over time, tattoos became associated with crime and rebellion, leading to stigma in modern society. Today, tattoos are widely embraced as an art form and personal expression, though some industries still view them negatively. Efforts to change perceptions continue, promoting tattoos as meaningful and legitimate creative expressions.
